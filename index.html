Overview

Fourier Epicycle Studio

Turn a portrait into a sparse Fourier epicycle: contours are traced, each loop gets a DFT, and the sum of circles redraws the subject. Use the tabs above to move between a short overview, upload & sliders, the four canvases, Desmos export, and the full-screen WebGL depth view. Defaults favor facial fidelity (more samples and harmonics on the largest paths).

Processing

105
After blur‑simplifying the image, edges use a percentile of Sobel strength. Slightly lower often keeps more of the face outline; higher = fewer, stronger edges.
3600
Total points split across paths. Higher = finer jawline / features and heavier FFT work.
280
Sparse Fourier terms per path. Raise for a closer match to the contour (especially portraits); lower for faster motion.
Largest contours (usually the face) get more harmonics so the epicycle hugs the subject.
1.0
How fast the epicycle phase sweeps through one full period (scaled by the FFT reference size).
